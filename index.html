The symbol, shape, and colour all say the same thing — dark navy 🛡 → blue ✓ → amber ◐ → hollow dashed ◇ → small grey ·, safest to least. No red/green, so it reads without distinguishing hues. Pins follow whichever dial you're using — GF, or vegan (🌱) when that's the active dial. Tap the 🌾 GF / 🌱 Vegan pills to relax one level at a time (long-press for the full guide).

This app summarises public info and is decision support, not a safety guarantee. For a serious allergy or celiac disease, always confirm cross-contamination handling with staff at the restaurant. Hours change and some places need reservations or sell out early.

🚉 Station survival

Show staff a card

Hand your phone to the staff — these explain the diet in Japanese, including the things that trip people up here (wheat hidden in soy sauce; fish dashi in almost everything).

Tip: turn screen brightness up so it’s easy to read.

📷 Scan any menu

Photograph a menu — even somewhere not in this app — and get what looks safe for gluten-free and vegan diners, plus questions to ask (English, Japanese & romaji).

🗣 Useful phrases

Point or read aloud — each phrase has the Japanese (with kana over the kanji to learn it) and romaji to pronounce it. Hand staff the phone if it's easier.

🏪 Konbini GF / Vegan

Decision support, not a guarantee — scan the label (原材料名) on every item. Pick a chain, then check the shopping lists.

📷 Read an ingredient label

Photograph the 原材料名 (ingredients) panel on any package — we’ll translate it and flag wheat/gluten & animal products. Decision-support, not a guarantee.

Greg has visited this place personally and is sharing it with care. These are not tourist destinations — they are beloved local spots that exist for the people who live nearby.

Before you continue, please read this:
• Maximum 4 people. A larger group overwhelms a small space and its regulars.
• Honour the cultural comfort level. Guide required means guide required. Don't improvise.
• Learn the minimum: おねがいします · ありがとうございました · すみません.
• If it's full, leave quietly. Don't wait at the door.
• You are a guest in someone's daily life. They were not expecting you.
By continuing you acknowledge that you will treat this place with the same respect you would want someone to show your own favourite neighbourhood spot.
