# KONBINI GF / VEGAN SURVIVAL GUIDE (Deeply Connected Dining)

Practical knowledge base for gluten-free/celiac and vegan travelers at Japan's three
major convenience-store chains — **7-Eleven (セブン-イレブン), FamilyMart (ファミリーマート),
Lawson (ローソン)**. Built from adversarially fact-checked research (round 1; ~20 verified
claims). Structured per-chain so it can power a map feature: tapping a konbini marker opens
that chain's guide.

⚠ POSTURE (non-negotiable, matches the app's liability stance): this guide is **decision
support, not a safety guarantee.** It never says a product "is safe." Celiac safety is
priority #1 — the guide teaches a verification *skill*, it does not certify products.
Konbini lineups also change seasonally, so we anchor on durable categories + the label
skill, not on specific SKUs.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
THE ONE SKILL: read the label for 小麦  [VERIFIED]
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Japanese law is the celiac's biggest asset here. As of 2025, **8 allergens are MANDATORY**
to declare on packaged food (特定原材料 / tokutei genzairyō):
  えび shrimp · かに crab · くるみ walnut · **小麦 WHEAT** · そば buckwheat · 卵 egg ·
  乳 milk · 落花生/ピーナッツ peanut.
→ **Because wheat (小麦) is mandatory, any packaged konbini food containing wheat MUST say
   小麦 on it.** Scanning the ingredient/allergen line for the kanji **小麦** is the single
   most reliable move a celiac can make in Japan. (Wheat has been mandatory since 2002.)

THE CATCH FOR VEGANS (and a celiac edge case): a second tier of ~20 allergens is
**recommended only, NOT required** (特定原材料に準ずるもの) — this includes **soy (大豆),
gelatin (ゼラチン), beef, pork (豚肉), chicken (鶏肉), mackerel (さば), salmon (鮭), squid**.
These may be present and **undeclared**. So the label is reliable for WHEAT, but you cannot
assume an animal ingredient is absent just because it isn't listed. Vegans must read the
full ingredient list (原材料名), not just the allergen line.

HOW TO READ / ASK (verified official phrasing):
  • Find the 原材料名 (ingredients) and アレルギー/アレルゲン (allergen) lines on the back.
  • Scan for 小麦 (wheat). For vegan, also scan 原材料名 for: 豚/牛/鶏 (pork/beef/chicken),
    魚/かつお/煮干し/だし (fish/bonito/sardine/dashi), 卵 (egg), 乳/バター/チーズ (dairy),
    ゼラチン (gelatin), はちみつ (honey).
  • Official allergy phrase to show staff:
    「私はアレルギーを持っています。この食品には〔小麦〕が含まれていますか？」
    (Watashi wa arerugī o motte imasu. Kono shokuhin ni wa [komugi] ga fukumarete imasu ka? —
    "I have allergies. Does this food contain [wheat]?")
  • Use a translation-app camera on the 原材料名 line — this is the authoritative method;
    konbini staff generally cannot vouch for trace/cross-contamination.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
GLUTEN-FREE: the reality  [VERIFIED]
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Why most konbini staples are NOT celiac-safe:
  • **Soy sauce (醤油) contains wheat** — so oden broth, bento sauces, teriyaki, tare,
    and most flavored items are out.
  • Fried/hot-case items (karaage, croquettes, fried chicken) are battered → wheat.
  • Bread/pastry aisle → wheat. Imitation crab, many sauces, instant noodles → wheat.
  • **No chain stocks certified gluten-free staples** (no GF bread/soy sauce/snacks). Nothing
    is certified GF; safety = self-service label reading via translation.

Plausibly OK as standard stock (ALWAYS label-check 小麦 each time — fillings vary by chain/season):
  • **Plain salted onigiri (塩むすび)** — the safest staple. BUT note some salted rice balls
    use vinegar (a possible gluten source) → still check.
  • **Umeboshi (梅) and sweet-bean/sesame (あんこ/ごま) onigiri** — noted wheat-free, but
    check (some salmon/tuna-mayo fillings contain soy sauce/wheat; e.g. certain salmon
    onigiri carry wheat).
  • ⚠ **Tuna-mayo onigiri often contains soy sauce → NOT GF.**
  • Plain steamed rice, plain edamame, boiled eggs (味付け marinated ones may use soy),
    plain cut fruit, plain nuts, plain yogurt — generally simpler, but verify.
  • Salads: may be OK *plain* but **dressings are almost never GF**; some "healthy" salads
    add unlabeled **barley (大麦)**; potato salad has vinegar → questionable.
  • Naturally GF drinks (water, tea, 100% juice, plain milk).

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
VEGAN: the reality  [PARTIALLY VERIFIED — see GAPS]
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Framework (verified): the hidden trap is that **fish dashi (かつお/煮干し) and animal
ingredients sit in the RECOMMENDED-only labeling tier** — they can be present and
undeclared. "Vegetable" items routinely contain fish dashi. So a vegan must read the full
原材料名, not trust the allergen line.

Plausibly vegan categories (NOT yet item-verified — treat as leads, read every label):
  • Umeboshi / kombu / sweet-bean onigiri (verify the rice/seasoning has no fish dashi).
  • Plain edamame, plain cut fruit, plain nuts, dark chocolate, certain potato chips.
  • Plain mochi / anko wagashi; soy milk and plant drinks; plain steamed rice.
  ⚠ Onigiri rice is sometimes cooked with dashi; inarizushi/seasoned items often use dashi;
    "salads" may contain ham/egg/fish. Verify each.

PER-CHAIN (this powers the map popup — tap a chain's marker → its section)  [VERIFIED]
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

All three chains publish allergen data online for the 8 mandatory allergens, and ALL three
explicitly state it is **a reference only, NOT a guarantee**, and warn of shared-equipment
cross-contamination. Self-service on-pack label reading is always the authoritative check.

▶ LAWSON (ローソン) — the strongest published data of the three.
  Official allergen portal declares **28 substances**: the 8 mandatory (特定原材料8品目,
  incl. 小麦 wheat) PLUS the 20 recommended items (準ずる20品目, incl. 大豆 soy, ゼラチン
  gelatin, beef, pork, chicken, etc.). So for a vegan, Lawson's portal is the most useful
  because it discloses many animal items the law doesn't require. Look for the
  Natural Lawson (ナチュラルローソン) health line for more plant-forward/簡単 options.
  Disclaimer: data is reference-only; confirm on-pack; consult a physician.

▶ FAMILYMART (ファミリーマート) — official allergen hub lists the 8 mandatory allergens
  (shrimp, crab, 小麦 wheat, buckwheat, egg, dairy, peanut, walnut). Reference-only;
  directs customers to verify the product label + in-store POP signage at purchase.

▶ 7-ELEVEN (セブン-イレブン) — publishes specified-allergen (特定原材料), nutrition, and
  calorie info via its official allergen/nutrition hub. Note (verified): 7-Eleven does NOT
  stock certified/clearly-labeled GF staples — no GF bread/soy sauce/snacks/pasta; rely on
  label reading. (Seven Premium private-label items still carry the mandatory 小麦 label.)

VEGAN — verified specifics:
  • **Plain salted onigiri (塩むすび / shio-musubi) is the ONE standard rice ball documented
    broth-free at all three chains** — the most reliable animal-free + GF rice ball. (Some
    use vinegared rice = acidity, not broth.)
  • **Ume (梅) and kombu (昆布) onigiri are NOT reliably vegan** — their seasoning often
    contains fish/bonito dashi that the law does NOT require to be labeled, so an "absent"
    allergen line does NOT mean dashi-free.
  • **Inarizushi is non-vegan at all three** (fish dashi in the seasoned tofu wrap).
  • Safer chilled-case picks: plain salad (NO dressing), salted edamame, plain tofu, and
    natto WITHOUT its sauce packet (the tare packet has wheat/soy). Nearly all complete
    bento meals are not vegan.

GF — verified additions/corrections (FamilyMart portal, primary):
  • FamilyMart kombu onigiri = wheat/sesame/soy; grilled-salmon = wheat/salmon/soy;
    cod-roe-mayo = wheat/egg/soy/**gelatin**. (Mayo fillings can hide gelatin.)
  • Reaffirmed: shio-musubi is the safest GF rice ball (check vinegar); tuna-mayo NOT GF
    (soy sauce); salad dressings NOT GF.

ROUND-3 — THE REAL SHOPPING LIST (abundance with verification)  [2026-06-24]
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
There IS a real konbini meal beyond salt onigiri — for both diets — but every item must be
confirmed on the 原材料名 (ingredient list). Nothing is certified; only the 8 mandatory
allergens are guaranteed-labeled.

GF SCAN LIST (the kanji that disqualify an item): 小麦 / 小麦粉 (wheat), 麦 / 大麦 (barley),
麦芽 (malt), 醤油 (soy sauce). Necessary but not sufficient for full celiac safety.

CROSS-CUTTING TRAP (both diets): hidden **katsuo / bonito fish dashi** — it is NOT even on
the 20-item recommended list and can be legally UNlabeled in small amounts. It contaminates
most onigiri fillings, many salads/dressings, pickles, and seaweed items. Assume dashi unless
proven otherwise.

⚠ CORRECTION (supersedes the round-2 hope that salada-chicken is an easy GF win):
**"Plain" サラダチキン is NOT reliably gluten-free — it's brand-dependent.** Seven Premium's
plain (糖質0g サラダチキン プレーン) contains 小麦粉 (wheat flour) + egg + dairy; Prima Ham's
plain is wheat-free. → must label-check the specific brand every time; flavored versions
assume soy sauce/wheat.

EGGS: plain **salt** hard-boiled egg (marked 塩, brined through the shell) = GF & needs no
sauce. AVOID brown marinated 味付け/煮たまご (soy-sauce based). Heuristic "brown = soy" (note:
some salt-egg brines use dashi-salt → GF but not vegan; eggs aren't vegan anyway).

──────── 🌾 CELIAC / GF KONBINI MEAL (scan 小麦/大麦/麦芽/醤油 on each) ────────
  • Plain SALT onigiri (塩むすび) — rice + salt.
  • Plain SALT hard-boiled egg (塩; not the brown marinated kind).
  • Plain tofu / 冷奴, edamame, natto WITHOUT its tare packet (the packet = wheat/soy).
  • Cut fruit & cut vegetables — UNdressed (dressings aren't GF; some "healthy" salads add barley).
  • Plain/salt/nori potato chips; plain nuts; dried sweet potato (干し芋); plain yogurt; milk.
  • サラダチキン ONLY if its label shows no 小麦 (Prima-Ham-type yes; Seven-Premium-type no).
  • Naturally-GF drinks (water, tea, 100% juice).
  ✗ Avoid: anything with 醤油, oden, all hot-case/fried, bread/pastry, dressings, imitation crab.

──────── 🌱 VEGAN KONBINI MEAL (read full 原材料名; beware unlabeled dashi) ────────
  • Plain SALT onigiri (the ONLY reliably animal-free onigiri — ume/kombu/natto/inari hide
    katsuo or niboshi dashi).
  • Edamame (incl. salted), plain tofu / 冷奴, natto WITHOUT the tare packet.
  • Fresh & cut fruit, plain undressed salads, some pickles (check dashi).
  • Nuts, dried fruit, salt/nori chips, dried sweet potato (干し芋), senbei (check 醤油),
    Natural Lawson health-line (e.g. yuzu-salt soybean chips).
  • Soy milk & plant drinks; dark chocolate (check dairy).
  ✗ Vegan traps: hidden katsuo dashi; 乳化剤 (emulsifiers, sometimes animal); salad dressings
    (chicken/fish extract); gelatin, honey, butter/margarine/creaming powder.

HONESTY CAVEAT: nothing here is certified GF/vegan — Japanese labels only certify "no
[mandatory allergen] as an ingredient," not facility cleanliness/thresholds, and
recommended-tier ingredients (soy, chicken, pork, fish/bonito dashi, gelatin) may be hidden.
Legal framework + the salad-chicken SKUs = PRIMARY sources; the broader lists = cross-
corroborated blog tier. Konbini KB is now COMPLETE (law + portals + shopping lists); only
fine store-layout remains soft.
